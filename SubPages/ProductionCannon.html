Production

First I imported a cube and merged all the vertices using (M) allowing me to start tracing the mesh. This was effective as it would allow me to capture little details which will add to the design and quality to the mesh.

I completed half of mesh however, needed to add a marker half way across the mesh this is because later steps would not work properly without this.

I added 2 new modifiers which will allow me to develop the cannon shape.

  • Screw - I used this modifier because it would create a spiral shape which can be 360 degrees allowing a full spiral shape for my cannon. The Axis is the way that the spiral shape would be along and axis object allowed me to use the marker positioning it perfectly to where I want it.
    • Subdivision - A single subdivision is mesh divided by 4. Since I am using 2 subdivisions I am diving the entire mesh by 8 this is because it allows a much more smoother and better shaded look. Allowing the quality to look a lot better.

However, with this modifiers there is one issue that being it doesn’t fill the entire front face for my mesh. I then decided to delete the faces in image 1 and edge selecting the inner edge to then grid fill to look like image 2. After than I ctrl E and extruded along normal allowing me to move the faces inwards.

I added a cube and positioned it to it matched the reference image. I also used a mirror modifier and used the marker as the mirror object. I continued to extrude the cube so the cube would reach the end,

I also bevelled the edge with ctrl B to give it a nice looking roundness.

I added a UV sphere and cut it in half and deleted the vertices which I did not need. I then extruded the edge down to the dotted line and used the mirror modifier again using the marker (not visible in image).

I then applied the modifiers and shaded them smooth.

I added 2 cubes and scaled the according to the blueprint. However, not all the way this is because I didn’t want to cause clipping within the mesh between the wheel.

I then started to work on the body however, decided that I didn’t want the bevelled edges and wanted something more sharp. I then extruded it according to the blueprint the best I could without bevelled the edges. This was more of a aesthetic choice for what I wanted to do.

I extruded past the trunnions (circle looking) then added a cylinder to represent the trunnions. However, this caused a issue as now we got a n-gon. I decided to extrude it along normal then delete the face and extrude and scale inwards a little then grid fill. With the outer edge I extruded it to add some more depth. I also cut in half and mirrored it with a subdivision.

This is what I got so far at this point of time. However, now it is time to add little details.

This I used the same one from image 10 but scaled it down. I then inserted a torus which I scaled to get the rings that I wanted.

I added 2 cylinders and scaled them to look like wheels that would fit for the cannon. This is when I stopped following the blueprint as I wanted to try and design my own wheels. I deleted all the faces and replaced for grid fill therefore, avoiding n-gons. I mirrored it the body and copy and pasted it across to the other placement of the wheels.

I added a cube and mirror it to the trunnions then got the edge on the top right hand side and moved it up and used image 10 again to make it look like it was bolted in

I also decided that I wanted to add detail to the front of the cannon. I decided to extrude along normal till it was further enough back then added a cylinder and made it a boolean so that I could make a lip around the cannon.
