Racing Game GDD

Concept

My game will is an open-world racing based in Tokyo, Japan. This will be a free-form exploration which you can participate in AI or multiplayer powered racing. The world will have a anime shaded theme which embraces anime culture. Explore the streets of Tokyo and climb through ranks or drive freely with no limits.

Game Objective

Main Objective

I am aiming to make this game so that people can unwind and take in the buildings which Tokyo has for those who want to visit. The purpose of this game is to provide players with an engaging open world racing game, allowing the player to relax or, if they want to, be competitive against smart AI cars or play with friends. The main objective is to race against AI or your friends and become the top of the leader board in every race. Or, alternatively, sit back and take in the views of the game.

Gameplay Goals

I want the player to experience the roads in Tokyo with a new look of anime to embrace the Japanese culture. I want the player to feel free with no limits and explore the map. But if they are feeling competitive then why not race with the AI or with others. These races are suppose to feel hard and challenging for the player to aim high on the leader boards but for those that don’t feel that competitive they can go with something more casual and relaxed and drive around the map. I want the player to react in different ways based on their mood when playing. For example, people who want to drive around the map might feel like following the speed limits or racing as fast as they can.

Presentation Goals

I want the players to feel immersive within the world as they are part of it. Taking in the scenery and beating their personal best. I want all assets to look like they belong in an anime and that the player is part of that anime show. In order to give this immersive goal I will need to consider audio, graphics, interface and more.

Game Engine

I will be using the Unreal Engine (5.6), which is full optimized and has many features which can be built upon it. This is because Unreal Engine has a plugin called Cesium which allows me to import google maps. I have also chosen Unreal Engine as I am more familiar with it. Unreal also comes with a free driving template, which I can use and refine in order to get a large majority of the game play done. However, I did consider unity and Godot, but they lacked the features which I wanted; such as visual scripting, which I am more familiar with.

Target Audience

Primary: Primary research often is first hand research which has not been done before. The benefits of primary is that it will be up to date and is more accurate towards my target audience.

Secondary: Secondary research is second hand research often being done by someone else and sources from Wikipedia or books which can be out of date.

Why is a research important

Research is important because it allows you to understand your target audience and who your game is targeted at. This will allows you to build own audience and target the game towards them.

Why is a audince important to your game

A audience is important because it allows you to promote your game in a more efficient way and that knowing your audience is will allow the game to be more driven towards them this can lead into changes with art style or game mechanics to beat suit the people who will be playing your game.

Gameplay

Mechanics

I plan on adding driving controller which can be a combination of realistic such as beam mg or Assetto Corsa which are well known games within the industry. I also plan on adding a racing system with advanced AI to simulate real time racing. I am also planning to add multiplayer. I also want the players to be able to customise their own cars

Story

The story is you are a low ranking rookie who seeks to take first place as you race along the streets in Tokyo. Win the cups and become the ultimate racing champion. Do you think you have what it takes?

UI

The UI of the game is going to be basic as I don’t want the player to feel overwhelmed when playing the game and having to look all over the screen for certain points of interest therefore, I will have a speedometer and a mini map during the actual game however, in pause menu there will be a lot more options such as customisation for the car and different ways of customisation the game itself such as what you want to appear in game.

Possible Hurdles

There a numerous hurdles in the way which I’ll have to overcome in order for this game to be developed.

  1. Quality not quantity. I want my game to be to a good quality not quantity this is because it is very hard to model good looking cars and buildings. It will also be by picking a starting point after the block out weather to do the map first or to model a few cars.
  1. AI. This could be as easy or hard as I want it to be this is because there are many different ways of going about AI weather to hard code it into blueprints or use a behaviour tree which can use more advanced algorithms in order to make them more realistic. Due to I want quality not quantity this means I’ll have to go down the behaviour trees and to use splines to help outline the track for the AI to follow.
  1. Multiplayer. This will require c++ and networking which I have done before therefore I lack experience and could be a major road block in the game and could halt development until I am more experienced.
  1. Authorisation of use of brand and engine. This is important as the cars will be inspired by their 1:1 replicas which could cause legal problems for the game development as companies could say no and forbids me to using their cars and engine noises. This is also a issue for use of radio stations.

I will over come these hurdles by separating each one into their own unreal engine project this will allow me to work on systems more effectively as I would not feel overwhelmed
