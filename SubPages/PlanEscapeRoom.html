Escape Room GDD

Overview (what is a escape room)

An escape room is when people are locked in a room and in order to escape they need to solve puzzles within a certain amount of time to accomplish typically finding the key to unlock the room. Sometimes there are also entity which can follow the the player around games such as escape simulator and granny are known examples of this.

Concept

Timmy is a regular boy who fell asleep one night into the dream world where he is trapped in a broken void world (the dream world). In order to wake up from the dream world he has to progress through 10 floors to meet with the dream capture where he will have to fight.

Puzzle interaction

physical based animations — this will be a active ragdoll making the character flop in strange and different ways which can make it easier or harder to the player for positioning objects and interacting with puzzles. Therefore, it adds a unique element to most puzzle interactions.

movable objects — this will be able to be moved by the player in order to get to higher ground or interact with the environment to benefit their needs. This is also going to allow players to destroy objects such as glass this will add to the puzzle element as player will need to think where to put the objects.

grind rails — this will allow the player to grind on rails to complete certain puzzles with timing a jump to get on to the correct rails. This will also add a fun perspective to an escape room.

My game will be inspired by both Human Fall Flat and Stumble Guys this is because I like the low poly stylised looked as it is simple to make. This also will go well with the puzzle interactions and mechanics.

I also plan on adding saturated lighting to give a more toon look this is because I think the combination will go well between the low poly stylised and cartoon. This can be made with flat shading and post processing.

Level design

Each level is presented with a different colour this is because that will be the primary colour for each of them. 1 - 10.

Each floor will have it’s own layout which will introduce different gameplay mechanics as you work your way up the ladder.

Each floor colour can also simulate a different biomes for example 6 can represent the sky due to it’s bright sky blue colour. Though there is no progression meaning behind the biomes I think this would be a cool twist to add to the game as it can have different settings for each.

There lacks design in the image as I don’t want to draw each floor by hand however, I am willing to do trial and error for the floors to be functional.

A layout is important because it can help the developer labels the points of interest and how the player will operate around the level and solve the level. Level layout is also important as it can give you a rough timing on how long it takes a player to complete your game as you don’t want it to finish too quickly but not too long as well. Developers often do this will grey box and detailed drawings of where they want things to be this is because it good for planning and understanding the game you want to make.
