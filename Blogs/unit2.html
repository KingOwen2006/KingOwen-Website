Surveys

Surveys are one way of primary research to get feedback or to gather information about an audience. Primary research is first-hand research that is done by you and is more reliable than secondary as that has already been done before and could be out of date.

A survey can vary depending on the results you want to get out of it. Anyone can take part in a survey, however, awareness depends on the scope of your audience and product. A survey can be done both on paper and online. For example, you could ask members of a Discord server, share on a Twitter page, or if using paper, ask people in person.

Types of questions

  • Closed questions — normally has 2 answers such as Yes/No.
  • Open questions — allows the audience to write their own detailed answer, which can be useful for feedback.

Surveys can sometimes result in biased opinions. However, if you make questions and choices cover both sides of an argument, you can achieve fair and unbiased results.

Game Research

BLACK CLOVER: QUARTET KNIGHTS

  • Peak player count: 84 players
  • User reviews: Mostly Positive — 71.58% SteamDB rating

This game is based on the anime Black Clover, following an alternative storyline as a team battle. The lower player count may be due to lack of advertising. Personally, the game felt unpolished, almost like a beta. Movement felt stiff, and visuals could have been improved. For example, character designs didn’t feel like they were taken directly from the anime, unlike The Seven Deadly Sins: Origin which looks more faithful to its source material.
